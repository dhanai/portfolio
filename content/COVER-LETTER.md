A quick note on what the resume can't show. I'm a founder, not a traditional engineering hire, and the thing I'd bring to this role isn't a credential — it's the habit of fixing whatever's in front of me. Building Takeout Order to $3.8M meant hand-drawing the catalog and learning whatever the work demanded next: Parse, then Firebase, then AngularJS and Ionic, then deciphering payment and shipping APIs by hand back when there was no AI to lean on. When the print machine died I learned to split-crimp wire; when it needed a teardown I pulled a hundred screws and remembered where each one went. The specific tools kept changing; the habit of relearning them didn't. That's why I trust myself with the parts AI can't own: the architecture, the craft, and the call on what ships. It's the same posture I'm building into Margenie now: capable, inspectable, nothing goes live until a human says so. It's the one I'd want to bring to Anthropic's web presence. I'd love the chance to help build it.
